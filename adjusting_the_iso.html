Adjusting the ISO

By adjusting the ISO, the camera sensor becomes more or less sensitive to light. DSLRs typically have an ISO range of 100 (low sensitivity) to 12,800 (high sensitivity). A high ISO will create a brighter photograph, with a grainer image. A low ISO will yield a sharper and darker image.

  1. Direct the camera lens at the desired subject and assess the lighting
  2. Adjust the ISO setting by pressing the button and highlighting an ISO option on the display screen
    1. For bright lighting, adjust the ISO until the display screen reads an ISO closer to 12,800
    2. For low lighting, adjust the ISO until the display screen reads an ISO closer to 100
