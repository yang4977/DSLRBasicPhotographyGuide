Modifying the Aperture

By modifying the aperture, you are widening or narrowing the camera lens. The aperture is measured by the f-stop. A higher f-stop (near f/2.8) will allow for a narrower lens and will create a darker image. A lower f-stop (near f/16) will allow a wider lens opening with more depth to the image.

  1. Simultaneously rotate the command dial and hold down the button
  2. Determine whether you would like a narrow or wide aperture and adjust accordingly
    1. For a narrow aperture, move towards an aperture of f/16
    2. For a wide aperture, move towards an aperture of f/2.8

When capturing a portait of a person, a wide aperture (or low f-stop) would be most desirable in order to blur the background and focus the camera lens on the person in the frame. Contrastingly, a scenic photograph would require a narrow aperture (or high f-stop) in order to adjust focus on the entire landscape.
