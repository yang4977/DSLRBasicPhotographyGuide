Determining the Shutter Speed

By determing the shutter speed, you are increasing or decreasing the camera's exposures to light. The duration of time is measured as fractions of a second. A faster speed of 1/200-second will create a darker image, whereas a slower shutter speed of ½-second will yield a lighter image.

  1. Rotate the command dial to adjust the shutter speed
  2. Determine whether you would like a fast or slow shutter speed and adjust accordingly
    1. Rotate to the right for a faster speed
    2. Rotate to the left for a slower speed

If a frame of movement is desired, a faster shutter speed would be optimal. For instance, if you would like to photograph someone running, this will require a faster shutter speed in order to capture more frames per second.
